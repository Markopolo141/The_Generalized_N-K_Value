\begin{abstract}  % put your abstract here!
We introduce a solution concept for generalized non-cooperative games called the \textit{generalized Neyman and Kohlberg value} (GNK value). 
We then apply the GNK value as a cost allocation mechanism for determining payments between agents in electrical optimal power flow (OPF) instances.
The GNK value provides a method of assigning value to agent's energy transfers that extends axiomatically from game theoretic solution concepts such as the Shapley Value and Nash-Bargaining solution.

In contradistinction to other solution concepts, the GNK value
rewards electricity system participants in proportion to their coalitional competitive positions for monetary compensation within the network, while exhibiting budget-balancedness and continuity with network parameters.
We illustrate with calculation on a small OPF instance under DC approximation, and then proceed to investigate techniques for overcoming the computational challenges applying GNK to larger instances.
